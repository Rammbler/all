Prayagraj - The City of Gods

Prayagraj, previously known as Allahabad, is a city in the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. It is one of the oldest cities in India and is known for its rich cultural, historical, and religious heritage.

  • Famous for the Kumbh Mela, held every 12 years.
  • Home to several temples, ghats, and historical monuments.
  • A significant center for education and learning.

Some of the must-visit places in Prayagraj are:

  1. Triveni Sangam - The confluence of three rivers (Ganga, Yamuna, and Saraswati).
  2. Allahabad Fort - A fort built by Emperor Akbar in 1583.
  3. Anand Bhawan - The ancestral home of the Nehru family.
  4. Khusro Bagh - A historical garden that houses three tombs.

Plan your visit to Prayagraj and explore the city's rich history, culture, and spirituality.
